Post-Natal Care

Post-Natal Care & Recovery

Your whole life has changed and there is no right or wrong way to feel right now. Some women feel overwhelmed at the idea of being a parent and managing the needs of a tiny baby; others feel more relaxed and assured.

It is natural to experience a rollercoaster of emotions thanks to your postpartum hormones — a combination of intense love, joy, uncertainty, sleep deprivation, and even boredom… all in the same 24 hours.

Physically, you may feel sore and tired. It takes about six weeks for your body to recover from pregnancy and return to normal. Give yourself time, relax whenever you can, and above all, celebrate the amazing job you have done.

In Hospital After Birth

Use your time in hospital to rest and recover, bond with your new baby, and learn to care for them. We will see you after birth to ensure you understand the delivery, answer any questions, and address any concerns.

  • Your pulse and BP
  • Uterus is contracting normally
  • Caesarean wound healing with no infection
  • Perineum stitches care
  • Emotional wellbeing and feeding support

Support Professionals

There are also a number of specialists on hand to provide assistance when you need:

  • Paediatrician: Monitors your baby’s health
  • Lactation Consultants: Help with breastfeeding
  • Physiotherapists: Help with back and pelvic floor exercises

At Your Six-Week Check

We’ll ensure your recovery is progressing well and provide guidance on emotional and physical health. This includes:

  • Checking bleeding, bowel and bladder function
  • Scar assessment and pap smear (if necessary)
  • Discuss future pregnancy and contraception plans
  • Evaluate emotional wellbeing and adjustment

You Can Help Your Body Recover By

Eating well and maintaining a balanced diet

Resting as much as possible

Limiting visitors to allow rest and bonding

Asking for pain relief if you need it

Using ice packs on stitches for comfort

Drinking water and eating fibre-rich food

Remember, if at any time you are worried about how you are feeling — physically or emotionally — talk to someone.
